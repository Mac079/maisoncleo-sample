Notre histoire — Our story

The fabric
comes first.
Everything else
follows.

MaisonCléo began as a request. A daughter asked her mother to sew her a silk top because she couldn't find what she wanted anywhere. That was the whole plan. What grew from it was something neither of them expected.

The beginning

A daughter. A seamstress.
One silk top.

Marie Dewet had worked internships inside the fashion industry and come out the other side disillusioned. She had seen the real cost of a polyester blouse. She had seen the margins. She had seen what went into the clothes people wore without question — and she stopped buying them.

She wanted handmade clothes, in natural materials, made locally. She couldn't find them at a price she could afford. So she asked her mother, Nathalie — Cléo — a qualified seamstress, to make her a little silk top. Then another. Then a few more.

Marie put them on Instagram. People asked to buy them. She built an e-shop. That was the beginning of MaisonCléo — not a business plan, not a pitch deck. A request between a daughter and her mother.

"I asked my mother, who is a professional seamstress, to make me little tops in silks because I wanted to buy only handmade clothes in natural materials. It was impossible to find what I wanted — or it was too expensive, or it wasn't made locally."

— Marie Dewet, Founder
The name

Maison. Cléo.

"Maison" because all the women in Marie's family were into sewing. The skill passed down through generations — from hands to hands, room to room. A house full of fabric and thread and the knowledge of how to turn one into the other.

"Cléo" because that is what everyone calls Nathalie. Not her formal name. Her name. The one her family uses, the one her daughter calls her. MaisonCléo is named for a person, not a concept — and that specificity is intentional.

The process

The fabric arrives.
Then we imagine.

MaisonCléo does not work from mood boards or seasonal trend reports. The inspiration comes from the fabric itself — deadstock from French Couture houses and factories, materials that existed before the idea of a garment did.

Marie finds a fabric. If she can picture what it wants to become, she buys it. If she cannot, she doesn't. That is the edit. Not a trend calendar. Not a sales forecast. Just a question of what a piece of silk or lace or cotton is asking to be.

i.

Source

Deadstock from French Couture houses. Surplus from French factories. Every fabric is natural. Every yard would otherwise go to waste.

ii.

Design

Marie imagines the piece from the fabric — not the other way around. No brief, no formula. The material decides what it becomes.

iii.

Make

Cléo and the atelier team make each piece by hand, to order, in the north of France. One hour to four and a half hours per garment, depending on the complexity.

iv.

Your size

Every piece is available made-to-measure. If a MaisonCléo garment should fit anyone who wants it, it will be made to fit anyone who wants it.

"The inspiration comes from the fabrics I find and receive, and from there, I imagine what can be done."

— Marie Dewet
